About Me

I am a data scientist and researcher passionate about clean energy technologies. With an MSc. in Financial Engineering from WorldQuant University (USA), a BSc. in Mathematics & Physics from Makerere University (Uganda) and a postgraduate diploma in Data Science & Machine Learning from MIT’s Institute for Data, Systems and Society, I blend quantitative rigour with domain expertise to deliver actionable insights.

My experience ranges from operations and project management to data analytics and monitoring & evaluation. I specialise in extracting insights from complex datasets and survey reports, designing monitoring and evaluation systems, conducting impact assessments and leveraging data science techniques for strategic decision‑making across industries. My goal is to harness data to advance sustainable energy solutions across Africa.

Projects Undertaken

1

Led the GIZ DRIVe 2.0 internship programme, delivering soft skills and work‑readiness training and coordinating placements with host organisations in Uganda’s e‑mobility and solar energy sectors.

2

Implemented clean cooking interventions in Kampala’s Kisenyi informal settlement, partnering with Enabling African Cities for Transformative Energy Access (ENACT) to improve access to modern cooking technologies.

3

Research associate in the Utilities 2.0 pilot in Kiwumu, Mukono district, supporting Power for All’s M&E work on decentralised renewable energy systems.

4

Project manager and monitoring & evaluation lead for the Modern Cooking Services (MECS) national supply‑chain competition, collaborating with EnerGrow and CREEC to scale up electric cooking in Uganda.

5

Oversaw EnerGrow’s local subsidy grant from GIZ to scale clean cooking in Uganda, managing the project from inception to completion.

6

Coordinated sales, data collection and M&E for the COSMO mini‑grid project on Lolwe Island with Equatorial Power, assessing feasibility of extending electric cooking to off‑grid areas.
